The Hidden Gems of Sustainable Travel

By: Jane Wambui | August 1, 2025

Why Sustainable Travel Matters

As global tourism grows, so does the impact on local environments and cultures. Sustainable travel isn’t just a trend—it's a movement toward preserving the places we love. At EcoTrails, we spotlight communities and destinations that practice responsible tourism.

1. Elgeyo Escarpment – Kenya’s Overlooked Treasure

Nestled in Kenya’s Rift Valley, the Elgeyo Escarpment offers breathtaking views and rich cultural experiences. Unlike the bustling Maasai Mara, this region remains untouched, with eco-lodges run by local families promoting community-driven tourism.

2. The Plastic-Free Shores of Lake Bunyonyi, Uganda

Lake Bunyonyi is known for its serene waters and eco-conscious practices. Travelers are encouraged to minimize waste and participate in community cleanups as part of their stay. This is low-impact tourism at its finest.

3. Sustainable Trekking in the Bale Mountains, Ethiopia

Forget over-touristed peaks—Ethiopia’s Bale Mountains offer high-altitude eco-trekking with a fraction of the foot traffic. Local guides, wildlife protection efforts, and zero-waste camping principles make this a must for conscious explorers.

Key Tips for Responsible Travel

  • Choose locally owned accommodations
  • Minimize single-use plastics
  • Respect cultural customs and language
  • Offset your carbon footprint
  • Leave no trace—pack out everything you bring in
